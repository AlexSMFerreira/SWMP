The first weeks were spent on the literature review (sensor selection, ocean-wave models and multisensor fusion) and on setting up the ROS~2 environment. Hands-on work began with early stereo and point-cloud experiments on a provided drone-flight dataset; when these had limited success on the sea surface, the pipeline was first validated on a simpler static-camera case using collected calibration data. From there, the bulk of the project was the search for a disparity-estimation backend that works on water: multiple classical and learned backends were implemented, tuned and compared, a thread that ran from the early experiments all the way to the final comparison campaign. In parallel, the pipeline was integrated behind a single launch command and georeferenced once the team supplied the calibrated navigation bags and the rig URDF geometry, including a roll/orientation correction driven by horizon detection. The wave-parameter estimators were then developed and cross-reconciled (the altimeter band-limiting fix, the point-cloud bad-frame gate, and the Doppler/encounter-frequency correction) before the final backend comparison, validation and report writing.
